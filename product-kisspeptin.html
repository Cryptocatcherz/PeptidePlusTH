History about Kisspeptin-10

Kisspeptin was discovered in 1996 in Hershey, Pennsylvania, and was initially named "metastin" for its role in suppressing cancer metastasis. However, its most significant discovery came in the early 2000s when researchers identified its critical role in reproductive function. Scientists found that mutations in the kisspeptin receptor (GPR54) caused hypogonadotropic hypogonadism, revealing kisspeptin as a master regulator of the reproductive hormone axis. Since then, Kisspeptin-10, the bioactive 10-amino acid C-terminal fragment, has been extensively studied for its role in puberty onset, fertility regulation, ovulation induction, and potential therapeutic applications in reproductive disorders. It has become a key focus in reproductive endocrinology research, with ongoing clinical trials exploring its use in assisted reproduction and hormone regulation.

Frequently Asked Questions about Kisspeptin-10

What is Kisspeptin-10 used for in research?

Kisspeptin-10 is primarily researched for its role in reproductive hormone regulation, GnRH pulsatility, fertility mechanisms, puberty onset, ovulation induction, and as a diagnostic tool for assessing HPG axis function. It's also studied in metabolic and cardiovascular research.

How should Kisspeptin-10 be stored?

What is the difference between Kisspeptin-10 and Kisspeptin-54?

Kisspeptin-10 is the bioactive C-terminal 10-amino acid fragment of the full 54-amino acid Kisspeptin-54 peptide. Both bind to the same receptor (GPR54/KISS1R) with similar potency, but Kisspeptin-10 is more commonly used in research due to easier synthesis and equivalent biological activity.

How quickly does Kisspeptin-10 work?

Kisspeptin-10 has a rapid onset of action. GnRH neurons respond within minutes, with peak LH levels typically observed 30-60 minutes after administration. The short half-life of ~28 minutes means effects are relatively brief, returning to baseline within 2-4 hours.

Can Kisspeptin-10 be used for fertility research?

Yes, Kisspeptin-10 is extensively studied in fertility research. It's being investigated for controlled ovarian stimulation, as an alternative trigger for oocyte maturation in IVF, and for diagnosing and treating hypothalamic amenorrhea and other reproductive disorders.

Does Kisspeptin-10 work in both males and females?

Yes, Kisspeptin-10 stimulates GnRH release in both male and female subjects, though the magnitude and downstream hormonal effects may differ based on sex, reproductive status, and endogenous hormone levels.

What is receptor desensitization and how can it be avoided?

Frequent, repeated administration of Kisspeptin-10 can lead to GPR54 receptor desensitization, reducing subsequent responses. This can be minimized by allowing adequate time between doses (several hours to days, depending on the research protocol) or using pulsatile rather than continuous administration.
