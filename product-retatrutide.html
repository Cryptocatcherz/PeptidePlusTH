Retatrutide side effects

As a triple agonist, Retatrutide may produce side effects similar to other metabolic peptides, primarily gastrointestinal in nature. The addition of glucagon receptor activation may result in slightly different side effect profiles compared to dual agonists. Most effects are dose-dependent and typically diminish with continued use.

Common Side Effects:

  • Nausea (especially during initial titration)
  • Decreased appetite and early satiety
  • Diarrhea or constipation
  • Vomiting (particularly at higher doses)
  • Abdominal discomfort or cramping
  • Fatigue during adjustment period
  • Injection site reactions
  • Headache

Less Common Effects:

  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Increased heart rate (typically mild)
  • Sleep disturbances
  • Hair thinning (with rapid weight changes)
  • Gastroesophageal reflux

Minimizing Side Effects:

  • Strictly follow the gradual titration protocol
  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals throughout the day
  • Maintain adequate hydration (2-3 liters daily)
  • Avoid high-fat, greasy, or spicy foods
  • Take with food if nausea occurs
  • Consider anti-nausea support during initial weeks
  • Monitor blood pressure and heart rate regularly
