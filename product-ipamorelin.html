History about Ipamorelin

Ipamorelin was developed in the late 1990s as part of research into selective growth hormone secretagogues. Unlike earlier GHRPs such as GHRP-6 and GHRP-2, which could increase cortisol and prolactin alongside growth hormone, Ipamorelin was engineered for exceptional selectivity. It binds specifically to the growth hormone secretagogue receptor (GHS-R1a) without cross-reactivity, resulting in pure GH stimulation. This breakthrough made Ipamorelin one of the most studied peptides in growth hormone research, valued for its clean pharmacological profile and minimal side effects.
