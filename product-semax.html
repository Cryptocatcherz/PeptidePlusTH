History about Semax

Semax was developed in the 1980s by Soviet researchers at the Institute of Molecular Genetics in Moscow. It is derived from a fragment of adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH 4-10) with modifications to enhance stability and bioavailability. The peptide was originally created to treat brain circulation disorders and has since been extensively researched for its cognitive-enhancing and neuroprotective properties. Semax works by modulating brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) and other neurotrophic factors, supporting neuroplasticity and cognitive function.
