What is Bacteriostatic Water?

Bacteriostatic Water for Injection (BAC Water) is sterile, non-pyrogenic water that contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol as a bacteriostatic preservative. The benzyl alcohol inhibits bacterial growth, allowing the vial to be used multiple times over an extended period while maintaining sterility. This makes it ideal for reconstituting lyophilized (freeze-dried) peptides and other injectable compounds used in research.

Why Use BAC Water for Peptides?

BAC Water is the gold standard for peptide reconstitution because it extends the shelf life of reconstituted peptides while maintaining sterility through multiple withdrawals. The bacteriostatic properties ensure that each injection remains uncontaminated, which is crucial for research integrity and safety. Most research peptides remain stable for 14-30 days when reconstituted with BAC Water and properly refrigerated.

How to use Bacteriostatic Water

BAC Water is used to reconstitute lyophilized peptides before injection. The process is straightforward but requires proper sterile technique to maintain the bacteriostatic properties and ensure research quality.

Reconstitution Instructions:

  1. Remove flip-top caps from both peptide vial and BAC Water vial
  2. Wipe rubber stoppers with alcohol swabs and allow to dry
  3. Draw desired amount of BAC Water into sterile syringe
  4. Insert needle into peptide vial at an angle against the glass wall
  5. Slowly inject BAC Water down the side of the vial - NEVER directly onto powder
  6. Swirl gently in circular motion - NEVER shake vigorously
  7. Allow to dissolve completely (may take 1-2 minutes)
  8. Solution should be clear and free of particles
  9. Refrigerate immediately after reconstitution

Storage and Handling:

  • Store unopened BAC Water at room temperature or refrigerated
  • Once opened, use within 28 days for optimal bacteriostatic efficacy
  • Always use sterile technique when withdrawing
  • Wipe rubber stopper with alcohol before each withdrawal
  • Never reuse needles or syringes
  • Discard if solution becomes cloudy or contains particles

Safety Information

Proper Sterile Technique:

  • Always work in a clean environment
  • Wash hands thoroughly before handling
  • Use alcohol swabs to disinfect vial stoppers before each access
  • Never touch needle tips or allow them to contact non-sterile surfaces
  • Use new, sterile needles and syringes for each withdrawal
  • Never inject BAC Water directly - use only for reconstitution

When NOT to Use BAC Water:

  • Do not use if solution is discolored, cloudy, or contains particles
  • Do not use if vial has been compromised or seal is broken
  • Do not use for neonatal applications (benzyl alcohol can be toxic to newborns)
  • Do not use past 28 days after first puncture
  • Some sensitive peptides may require sterile water instead - check specific peptide guidelines

Benzyl Alcohol Information:

Benzyl alcohol at 0.9% concentration acts as a bacteriostatic preservative and is generally well-tolerated. However, some individuals may have sensitivity to benzyl alcohol. If irritation or adverse reactions occur, consider using preservative-free sterile water instead.

Frequently Asked Questions about BAC Water

What is the difference between BAC Water and Sterile Water?

BAC Water contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol as a preservative, allowing multi-dose use for up to 28 days. Sterile Water contains no preservative and should be discarded within 24-48 hours after opening. For research requiring multiple injections, BAC Water is strongly preferred.

How much BAC Water should I use to reconstitute my peptides?

This depends on the peptide dosage and desired concentration. Common ratios are 1-3ml per 5mg peptide vial. Check specific peptide instructions or use online peptide calculators to determine optimal reconstitution volume for your research protocol.

How long does BAC Water last after opening?

When using proper sterile technique, BAC Water maintains bacteriostatic properties for up to 28 days after first puncture. Mark the date of first use on the vial and discard after 28 days.

Can I use BAC Water for all peptides?

BAC Water is suitable for the vast majority of research peptides. However, a few sensitive peptides may require preservative-free sterile water. Always check specific peptide reconstitution guidelines for best results.

How should reconstituted peptides be stored?

Peptides reconstituted with BAC Water should be stored in the refrigerator at 2-8°C. Most peptides remain stable for 14-30 days when properly refrigerated. Never freeze reconstituted peptides as this can denature the peptide structure.

Is one 30ml vial enough for multiple peptide vials?

Yes, one 30ml vial can reconstitute many peptide vials. For example, if you use 2ml per peptide vial, you can reconstitute up to 15 peptide vials from a single 30ml BAC Water vial.

Does BAC Water need to be refrigerated?

Unopened BAC Water can be stored at room temperature. Some researchers prefer to refrigerate after opening, though it's not strictly necessary as long as proper sterile technique is maintained. Refrigeration may extend usability slightly beyond 28 days.
