History about Selank

Selank was developed in the late 1990s by the Institute of Molecular Genetics of the Russian Academy of Sciences. It is a synthetic analog of the naturally occurring immunomodulatory peptide tuftsin, with modifications that enhance metabolic stability and central nervous system penetration. Selank was created to provide anxiolytic effects without the sedation, addiction potential, or cognitive impairment associated with traditional benzodiazepines. The peptide works through multiple mechanisms including modulation of serotonin, dopamine, and GABA systems, as well as enhancing BDNF expression and reducing inflammatory markers associated with stress.
