Somatrogon side effects

Somatrogon shares a similar side effect profile with traditional growth hormone, though the extended-release nature may result in more sustained effects. Most side effects are dose-dependent and reversible with dose reduction. Weekly dosing may reduce peak-related side effects compared to daily GH.

Common Side Effects:

  • Injection site reactions (mild, typically resolves quickly)
  • Peripheral edema (fluid retention)
  • Joint or muscle pain (especially during initial weeks)
  • Headache
  • Paresthesia (tingling sensations)
  • Mild elevations in blood glucose

Less Common Effects:

  • Carpal tunnel-like symptoms
  • Insulin resistance (monitor blood glucose)
  • Gynecomastia (rare, at higher doses)
  • Fatigue during dose adjustment period
  • Increased appetite
  • Antibody formation (rare but possible with fusion proteins)

Minimizing Side Effects:

  • Start with conservative weekly doses
  • Allow 4-6 weeks for tolerance development
  • Monitor IGF-1 levels to guide dosing
  • Regular blood glucose monitoring
  • Ensure adequate hydration
  • Reduce dose if significant side effects persist
  • Consider dose adjustment based on individual response
