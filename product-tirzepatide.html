Tirzepatide side effects

The most common side effects observed in research are gastrointestinal in nature, particularly during the initial titration phase. These effects are typically mild to moderate and tend to decrease over time as tolerance develops. The dual agonist mechanism may produce slightly different side effect profiles compared to pure GLP-1 agonists.

Common Side Effects:

  • Nausea (most common, usually temporary)
  • Decreased appetite
  • Diarrhea or constipation
  • Vomiting (especially at higher doses)
  • Abdominal discomfort or bloating
  • Fatigue during initial weeks
  • Injection site reactions

Less Common Effects:

  • Headache
  • Dizziness
  • Increased heart rate (mild)
  • Hair thinning (with rapid weight loss)

Minimizing Side Effects:

  • Follow gradual titration schedule strictly
  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals
  • Stay well hydrated throughout the day
  • Avoid high-fat, greasy foods
  • Take with food if nausea occurs
  • Consider anti-nausea support if needed
