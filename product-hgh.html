History about Human Growth Hormone

Human Growth Hormone was first isolated from cadaver pituitary glands in the 1950s. In 1985, the development of recombinant DNA technology allowed for synthetic HGH production, revolutionizing growth hormone therapy and research. Today, recombinant HGH is widely used in research settings to study growth, muscle development, metabolic function, recovery, and anti-aging processes. It remains one of the most studied hormones in medical research.

How to use HGH

HGH is administered via subcutaneous or intramuscular injection. The peptide should be reconstituted with bacteriostatic water or sterile water and stored properly once mixed. Injections are typically performed daily, with timing varying based on research protocols (often morning or before bed). Proper injection technique and site rotation are essential for optimal results.

Reconstitution Instructions:

  1. Use bacteriostatic water for reconstitution
  2. Add 1ml of water slowly to the 10IU vial
  3. Swirl very gently along the vial walls - NEVER shake
  4. Store in refrigerator at 2-8°C immediately after reconstitution
  5. Use within 14 days of reconstitution if using bacteriostatic water
  6. Inspect solution for clarity before each use
  7. Allow to reach room temperature before injection (10-15 minutes)

HGH side effects

While HGH is generally well-tolerated in research settings, some side effects may be observed, particularly at higher doses or with extended use. Most side effects are dose-dependent and reversible upon dose reduction or discontinuation.

Common Side Effects:

  • Mild joint pain or stiffness (especially initially)
  • Edema (water retention in extremities)
  • Carpal tunnel-like symptoms
  • Injection site reactions
  • Numbness or tingling in extremities
  • Headache

Less Common Effects:

  • Elevated blood sugar (insulin resistance)
  • Increased appetite
  • Gynecomastia (rare, at high doses)
  • Fatigue (usually temporary)
  • Nausea

Minimizing Side Effects:

  • Start with low doses and gradually increase
  • Split higher doses into multiple daily injections
  • Monitor blood glucose levels regularly
  • Ensure adequate hydration
  • Consider cycling protocols for long-term research
  • Reduce dose if significant side effects occur
